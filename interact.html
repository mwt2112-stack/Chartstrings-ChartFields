Post-Award Management
A practical guide to managing sponsored projects after the award is received — from account setup through closeout. Covers responsibilities of Sponsored Projects Finance (SPF) and the Research Policy & Indirect Cost Group (RPIC).
🏛️
Post-award financial administration is managed through the Office of the Controller and is carried out by two specialized groups: Sponsored Projects Finance (SPF) and the Research Policy & Indirect Cost Group (RPIC). Department administrators work directly with SPF for day-to-day project management.
The 8-Stage Sponsored Projects Lifecycle
1
Getting Started
Initial orientation to the sponsored project. Understanding sponsor requirements, compliance obligations, and team assignments. Your SPA Financial Analyst (FA) is assigned at this stage.
2
Initiation & Execution
Award account is established in InfoEd and fed to ARC. The budget is finalized, the chartstring is created (SPONS PC Business Unit with GG or PG project prefix), and the project is ready for spending.
3
Transacting
Day-to-day purchases, payroll charges, and journal entries are posted against the project. All transactions must comply with sponsor terms, OMB Uniform Guidance, and University policy.
4
Financial Reporting & Monitoring
SPF prepares and submits financial reports to sponsors. Departments conduct quarterly financial reviews, track budget variances, and monitor expenditures through ARC reporting tools.
5
Documenting Personnel Costs (Effort Reporting)
Effort certifications document the percentage of time faculty and staff spent on the project. RPIC processes annual Effort Certifications. Accurate effort reporting is a federal compliance requirement.
6
Receivables Management
SPF manages the invoicing cycle, cash application, and collections from sponsors. Payments from government sponsors are received electronically via Wells Fargo.
7
Closeout
After the project period ends, SPF performs final reconciliation, submits closing financial reports, and terminates the project account. Departments must ensure all allowable costs are posted before closeout.
8
Audit
The University is subject to federal audits (Single Audit under Uniform Guidance). RPIC prepares the annual Uniform Guidance Report. All sponsored project records must be retained per sponsor and University policies.
Key Administrative Offices
SPF
Sponsored Projects Finance
Handles the financial operations of sponsored awards from setup through closeout. Department administrators work directly with their assigned SPF Finance & Compliance Manager.
  • →Preparation & submission of financial reports to sponsors
  • →Preparation & submission of sponsor invoices
  • →Review and reporting of cost sharing and cost transfers
  • →Receivables management and cash application process
  • →Final reconciliation and closeout of terminated projects
RPIC
Research Policy & Indirect Cost Group
Oversees compliance with University policies and federal regulations governing post-award financial administration of sponsored programs.
  • →Monitoring sponsor agency policies & facilitating compliance
  • →Developing F&A rate proposals & negotiating with government agencies
  • →Publishing GRA tuition, fringe benefit, and recharge center rates
  • →Processing annual Effort Certifications
  • →Preparing the annual Uniform Guidance Report
SPA
Sponsored Projects Administration
The institutional authority to legally bind Columbia in grants administration. SPA reviews all grant applications before sponsor submission and manages award setup in ARC.
  • →Review and approval of all grant proposals before submission
  • →Assigned SPA Project Officers per department for proposal review
  • →Award account setup in InfoEd, feeding data to ARC
  • →Negotiation and execution of sponsored agreements & contracts
  • →Subaward preparation, review, and execution
Proposal Preparation (Pre-Award)
The Sponsored Projects Administration (SPA) office reviews all grant applications before submission to sponsors. SPA is the institutional authority to legally bind Columbia in grants administration. Every department is assigned a SPA Project Officer who reviews applications for completeness and budget accuracy.
TopicDescription
PI EligibilityFaculty and certain staff must meet Columbia's Principal Investigator eligibility requirements before submitting a proposal.
RASCAL PTAll proposals are registered in RASCAL Proposal Tracking (PT), which must be electronically signed by all relevant parties before submission.
Budget PreparationBudgets must comply with sponsor guidelines, include allowable costs, and properly account for F&A (indirect cost) rates negotiated by RPIC.
NIH / NSF SubmissionsEach agency has specific submission portal requirements. SPA Project Officers guide departments through agency-specific rules.
Subawards (Pre-Award)Proposed subawards to collaborating institutions must be included in the prime proposal with a scope of work and budget justification.
Data Management PlansMany sponsors (NSF, NIH) require a Data Management Plan describing how research data will be stored, shared, and preserved.
Award Setup
After receipt of a new award or non-competing continuation, the SPA Financial Analyst prepares the account for spending. The project is set up in InfoEd and the information is fed to ARC, where the chartstring becomes available for transactions.
Documents Required for Account Setup
✓Notice of Award (NOA) or fully executed contract
✓The original grant application / proposal
✓Finalized RASCAL Proposal Tracking (PT) record — electronically signed, with current FCOI disclosure
✓FCOI certification from subcontractors or consultants (if applicable)
✓IRB, IACUC, or other compliance office approval notice (if applicable)
✓Subcontract agreements, if subawards are included in the award
⚠
Chartstring Note: Sponsored projects use PC Business Unit = SPONS and a project number with a GG (Government Grant) or PG (Private Grant) prefix. The PCBU must align with the project prefix — see the Reference tab for the full alignment table.
Subawards
Pre-Award: Proposing Subawards
  • •Include subrecipient scope of work and budget in prime proposal
  • •Register subaward in RASCAL PT before proposal submission
  • •Obtain FCOI certification from proposed subcontractor
  • •SPA Project Officer reviews and approves subaward terms
  • •NIH foreign subawards require additional documentation per NOT-OD-25-130
Post-Award: Subrecipient Monitoring
  • •Departments must monitor subrecipient performance and financial reports
  • •RPIC oversees compliance with the Sponsored Projects Subaward Policy
  • •Subrecipient invoices are reviewed before payment is processed
  • •Subcontract expenses are coded to account 65020 (Subcontracts Out of MTDC)
  • •Annual risk assessments determine monitoring intensity

Financial Reporting & Monitoring
Financial reporting and monitoring ensures expenditures are timely, allowable, and compliant. Departments are expected to review project finances regularly and work with SPF to satisfy all reporting obligations to sponsoring agencies.
Quarterly Financial Review
Departments review outstanding transactions and budget balances each quarter using ARC and the Monthly/Quarterly WebViewer.
Annual Expenditure Validation
Year-end review confirming that all posted expenditures are allowable, allocable, and correctly recorded per sponsor terms.
Effort Reporting
Personnel costs on sponsored projects must be certified annually. RPIC coordinates the Effort Certification process across departments.
Budget Periods & Variances
Each award has defined budget periods. Overspending a budget category may require sponsor approval or rebudgeting before costs can be transferred.
Invoicing & Financial Reports
SPF prepares and submits interim and final financial reports and invoices to sponsors per the terms of each award.
Rebudgeting
Transferring budget between cost categories within an award. Some sponsors require prior approval; check award terms before rebudgeting.
Program Income
Revenue generated from activities supported by a sponsored award (e.g., conference fees, service fees). Must be tracked and reported per sponsor requirements.
Indirect Costs (F&A)
F&A rates are negotiated by RPIC with the federal government. Rates vary by project type. Applied automatically to the Modified Total Direct Costs (MTDC) base.
